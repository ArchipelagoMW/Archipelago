# Minecraft Fabric

## What is Archipelago?

"Archipelago is a cross-game modification system which randomizes different games, then uses the result to build a single unified multi-player game. Items from one game may be present in another, and you will need your fellow players to find items you need in their games to help you complete your own."[1]

For more information on how Archipelago works, or things that it does (information that is useful to know when using this mod), you can check out Archipelago's official FAQ.

## How does the Randomizer affect Minecraft

In order to randomize Minecraft, this mod locks various abilities and recipes behind Archipelago Items, with the completion of locations being the only way to get them back. There are options that the player can check that enable/disable Archipelago Items in order to change the difficulty of the game.

### Locations

The main locations added by this mod are advancements. Every time the player unlocks an advancement, an item will be sent out to Archipelago. Some additional advancements are also added to the game to add more for the player to do to get checks, all of which are pulled directly from old advancements and Bedrock achievements.

There are additional optional locations that can be enabled by toggling "Itemsanity" on, which makes it so most blocks and items that are obtainable in Survival grant checks (there are exceptions for some hard to reach items).

Based on Settings in the Randomizer, some locations will be excluded. Locations that can be excluded can be categorized as "Hard", "Exploration", or "Unreasonable". A full list of locations that can be excluded can be found HERE.

Modded Locations aren't currently supported, but are planned to be added in the near future.

### Items

There are multiple things that are added by the mod as Archipelago Items. Some items unlock things needed for progression, while others just give the player things that may or may not be helpful.

The list of items includes:

* Recipes - Recipes for various items required for progression, such as tools and weapons.
* Abilities - Abilities that the player normally has, such as Jumping, Sprinting, and Swimming
* Experience - Grants the player an amount of levels or experience points
* Items - Grants the player a random amount of an Item, such as planks or ore.
* Traps - Unwanted items that can optionally be added to the game. They do things such as reversing controls, enabling ice physics, or spawning lit TNT.


The Randomizer currently adds Archipelago Items for Vanilla Minecraft, but Modded Archipelago Items are planned for the near future.
